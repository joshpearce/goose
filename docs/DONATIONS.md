# Donations (optional)

**Goose is free, and it stays free. Nothing here is a paywall.**

Goose is a self-hosted biometric platform for WHOOP straps. It pairs directly with the strap over Bluetooth, stores everything locally in SQLite on your own device, and computes recovery, strain, HRV, and sleep on-device — with no WHOOP account and no cloud dependency. Every feature is included: live data, historical sync, the AI coach, and the self-hosted server upload. There is no "pro" tier, no trial, and no locked screen.

This page exists because people asked how to chip in. If Goose is useful to you and you *want* to, you can. If you don't, nothing changes and you'll never be asked twice.

**Honestly, though — if you can, please do.** Keeping Goose free and off the cloud means there's no company and no investor behind it. A little support decides whether deeper protocol work, Android improvements, and new features keep coming. **Crypto is the only way to contribute, on purpose:** staying anonymous rules out PayPal, Patreon, or anything with a name attached. That's not a hurdle — it's quick, global, and private for both of us.

> **Not affiliated with WHOOP.** Goose is an independent, unofficial interoperability project. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to WHOOP, Inc. "WHOOP" is used only to identify the hardware Goose talks to. **Goose is not a medical device** — derived metrics are approximations, not clinical data.

## Why donate at all?

You don't have to, and the project is built so you never need to. But the work is ongoing and unpaid, and donations go toward more of it:

- **Deeper protocol coverage** — extending historical sync, new biometric fields, and firmware-version parity as WHOOP ships updates.
- **Android client** — bringing the same local-first experience to Android alongside iOS.
- **Test hardware and time** — straps, devices, and the hours to keep reverse-engineering current as firmware changes.

Donations are a thank-you, not a transaction. Nothing about the app, your data, or your access depends on them.

## New to crypto? Here's the 2-minute version

You don't need to "be into crypto" to chip in:

1. **Install a mainstream exchange app** — Coinbase, Binance, Kraken, or **Cash App** (Cash App buys and sends Bitcoin directly, no extra app).
2. **Buy some Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), or Solana (SOL)** — a suggested **$20–$50** (a fraction of a year's WHOOP subscription); anything is appreciated.
3. **Tap Send / Withdraw**, paste the matching address below, and confirm.

A few notes so nothing goes wrong:

- **Only ever send a coin to its own network** (BTC to the BTC address, ETH to the ETH address, SOL to the SOL address). Sending across networks can lose the funds.
- **Copy the address in full** and double-check the **first and last few characters** — transactions are irreversible.
- Exchanges charge a small network fee; that's normal and goes to the network, not here.

## What donations are *not*

- **Not a subscription.** One-off, whenever you like, never recurring.
- **Not required.** Every feature works fully without paying anything.
- **Not tied to your data.** Goose has no server and no account by default. There is nothing to unlock and no record of who has or hasn't donated.

If you'd rather contribute without money, that's just as welcome: open an issue, file a bug, send a pull request, or help test on hardware you own. That kind of help moves the project forward as much as anything.

**Questions or bug reports:** [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/tigercraft4/goose/discussions) or [Discord](https://discord.gg/EyZE6gzAF2).

## A note on the project

Goose is built on prior community reverse-engineering — chiefly [`b-nnett/goose`](https://github.com/b-nnett/goose) for the WHOOP 5.0 BLE and Rust architecture, and [`NoopApp/noop`](https://github.com/NoopApp/noop) for BLE pairing patterns and protocol depth. It exists so that someone who owns a WHOOP strap can read their own biometric data from their own device, on infrastructure they control. Keeping it free and self-hosted is the whole point.

Thank you for even reading this far.
